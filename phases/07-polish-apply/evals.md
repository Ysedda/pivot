# Phase 7 — Evals

**Passing bar:** README skim test passes for every repo + at least 2 blog posts published + first 5 applications sent + 1 first-round interview booked. ~3 hours of eval time excluding the wait for interview replies.

## Drill 1 — README skim test (target: 10 min per repo)

For every repo / sub-directory in this lab:

1. Send the repo URL (no extra context) to a friend or peer.
2. Give them 5 min.
3. Ask them to write back: (a) what is this, (b) what problem does it solve, (c) what's one technical decision you made.

**Pass:** they get all three correct, in their own words.
**Partial:** they get two of three, or need clarification.
**Fail:** they couldn't tell — README needs another pass.

## Drill 2 — Cover-letter signal test (target: 5 min)

Send your cover letter draft and CV to someone in hiring (recruiter friend, manager you know). Give them 60 seconds.

Ask: "If you were screening for an SRE role, would you want to interview this person? Why or why not? What's the strongest single line?"

**Pass:** they identify a specific deliverable from this lab as the strongest signal, and the answer to "interview?" is yes.
**Partial:** yes-with-edits.
**Fail:** "I'm not sure what they actually do" — go rewrite.

## Drill 3 — Blog post peer review (target: 30 min)

For each of your 2–3 blog posts:

1. Get one peer (technical) and one outsider (smart non-engineer) to read it.
2. Technical peer should find at most one factual nit.
3. Outsider should be able to summarize the takeaway in one sentence.

**Pass:** both criteria met for each post.
**Partial:** one post fails one of the two checks.
**Fail:** otherwise.

## External attestation — the real one

- ≥5 applications sent to companies on your target list.
- ≥1 first-round interview booked.
- Bonus: ≥1 hiring manager replied via warm intro after blog post.

If you've shipped phase 7 and not got a single first-round interview within 4 weeks, the bottleneck is either the CV/cover letter (re-run Drill 2) or the target list (refresh: are you applying to roles that match the lab you built?).

## Whiteboard

The lab itself should be your whiteboard at this point. Be ready to walk an interviewer through any one of: phase 0 bootstrap, phase 2 CNI, phase 3 Ansible role design, phase 5 SLO, in <10 min, on a video call without screen-share. (Practice this. It's the most under-prepared interview moment.)

## Retention check

After landing the first role, schedule a 6-month retro: which phases held up, which faded, what you wish you'd skipped. Append to `lessons.md` — closes the program meaningfully.
